Dollar Neutral, to keep long position and short positions equal, or in a fixed range, let's say long position not bigger than short position 5%, if it is, this need to be rebalanced.


Volatility Targeting, for overall portfolio volatility target to a number, for example, my portfolio is QQQ, BIL and GLD, they have different volatility (based on 30 day data or 7 days etc), let's say 40, 1, 30, and targeting having a overall 25 volatility, then we should have less QQQ, but more BIL, based on this to reach a portfolio asset allocation to meet the target volatility


Beta Neutral, similar to Dollar Neotral, but instead of targeting value neutral but on beta neutral. A beta neutral strategy is an investment approach that aims to create a portfolio with a net beta of zero, meaning it is uncorrelated with market movements. This is achieved by balancing long and short positions in such a way that their overall volatility offsets each other, allowing the portfolio to profit from stock selection rather than market direction.
